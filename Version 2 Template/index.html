Hello World!

My name is Jay, and I'm currently studying Computer Science at the University of California, Los Angeles.

I grew up in the San Francisco Bay Area (Santa Clara, Cupertino, and San Ramon) and was surrounded by large technology companies my entire life. Determined to get a better understanding of the framework that drove these companies, I decided to sign up for an introductory college computer science class at San Jose State University when I was 16.

After getting exposed to the abstract yet beautiful world of problem solving defined through Computer Science, I was hooked.I've been programming ever since, and even though there are times when I get flustered due to annoying bugs, I think that incrementally creating something useful out of nothing is truly a surreal experience.

In my free time, I really like to exercise (tennis, table tennis, and weights), catch up on TV shows (current favorites are Friends, The Office, House of Cards, and The Big Bang Theory), read (I read everything from blogs to books, currently in love with Quora), and hang out with friends.

I'm always open to working on interesting projects, so if you have an idea that you need help creating, feel free to reach out to me!

BruinTime

Course Management. Simplified

New

BruinTime is a comprehensive grade distribution website (which is currently using dummy data). It uses a LAMP stack for the backend and JavaScript/jQuery/CSS/HTML for the front end. A MySQL database holds all of the grade records on an AWS server, and PHP is used to access those records. AJAX calls are used to present the data to the user on the front end.
